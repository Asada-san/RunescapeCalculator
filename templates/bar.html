Revolution Bar V1.05.3

Look through the combat sections for the abilities you want to put on the bar. If you want more information about a certain ability, hold CTRL and click on it, this will open up its RS3 wiki page.

Constructing the revolution bar is as simple as:

  • Clicking on abilities to make them appear in the first available slot on the bar.
  • Dragging them onto a slot.
  • Left clicking on an ability on the bar removes it.
  • The "clear bar" button removes all abilities from the bar.
  • Switching abilities on the bar can be done by dragging and dropping.

Also, have a look at the options section for perks, boosts and much more.

When you've set up your desired bar with the proper options, you should click on the FIGHT! button. A fight between a player and a dummy will now be simulated resulting in an Average Ability Damage Per Tick (AADPT) output (in both a number and a percentage of the base ability damage as calculated on the rs3 wiki). Clicking on this resulting number will allow you to view some more interesting information.

If anything is wrong or if you have any suggestions please let me know by contacting me on reddit: u/FTWmickyWTF or ingame: Asada-san. {# sending an email to: RS-RevolutionCalc@outlook.com.#}

  • The more damage boosting options are used, the more probable it is that the output damage is wrong due to orders in which these are applied. If you notice any errors, please let me know! (see general info for places to contact me)
  • Average ability damages are used for the calculating the AADPT.
  • Tuska's Wrath does its normal damage only. When fighting slayer creatures, please don't let revolution activate this ability, but instead activate it manually once every 2 minutes.
  • As the resulting A --> Ability <-- DPT suggests, auto attacks are not taken into account.
  • All ability information used to determine the AADPT is summarized on a spreadsheet.
  • The code (including html, js, css and python files) have been uploaded to Github. You're free to use it however you want.
  • All ability images were yoinked from the RS3 wiki.

Python Script

Level Boost: The amount of boosted levels (above your base level) due to potions and/or aura's (min:0, max:60).

Level 20 Gear: Assumes the equipment levels of all augmented items are equal to level 20.

Flanking Perk: Assumes the target is not facing you at all times.

Ruthless Perk: Assumes the player starts attacking with a full stack (5) and the stack is never reset.

Efficient: Assumes the player breaks channeling abilities such that they still do all their damage (except for Concentrated Blast which cuts off after 2 hits) but the next ability is activated earlier.

Switcher: Assumes the player switches between 2h/dual/shield whenever required for the next available ability.

Ability Damage: The ability damage as calculated in the "Calculating ability damage" section on the RS3 wiki (Min:1, Max:10000).

Stationary: Assumes the dummy is stuck in place, meaning bleed damage won't be multiplied. When not ticked, bleed damage is multiplied for all hits (unless the dummy is stunned and/or bound).

Stun&Bind Immune: Assumes the dummy is immune to stuns and binds from abilities.

Targets: Number of targets within reach for all AoE abilities.

Simulation T(s) and Start Adrenaline When a value has been set for both the Simulation Time (in seconds, min:1, max:3600) and the Starting Adrenaline (min:0, max:100), the Python script won't detect cycles but instead runs for the given simulation time and determines the Damage Per Tick (DPT) in that time.

Print more info: When ticked, up to the first 5 minutes of the simulation time can be verified upon clicking the resulting AADPT number (mostly used for debugging purposes).
