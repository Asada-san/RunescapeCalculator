Revolution Bar V1.03

Look through the combat sections for the abilities you want to put on the bar. If you want more information about a certain ability, click on it and it will link you to its RS3 wiki page.

Constructing the revolution bar is as simple as dragging abilities onto it. Deleting an ability from the bar is done by clicking on it, deleting the entire bar at once is done by clicking the "clear bar" button. You can also switch around abilities already on the bar. Lastly, have a look at the options section for potential perks, boosts and much more.

When you've got your desired bar, click on the FIGHT! button. A fight between a player and a dummy will be simulated resulting in an Average Ability Damage Per Tick (AADPT) output (in both a number and a percentage of the base ability damage as calculated here). Clicking on this resulting number will allow you to view some more interesting information.

If anything is wrong or if you have any suggestions please let me know by sending an email to: RS-RevolutionCalc@outlook.com.

Python Script

Level Boost: The amount of boosted levels (above your base level) due to potions and/or aura's (min:0, max:60).

Level 20 Gear: Assumes the equipment levels of all augmented items are equal to level 20.

Flanking Perk: Assumes the target is not facing you at all times.

Efficient: Assumes the player breaks channeling abilities such that they still do all their damage (except for Concentrated Blast which cuts off after 2 hits) but the next ability is activated earlier.

Switcher: Assumes the player switches between 2h/dual/shield whenever required for the next available ability.

Ability Damage: The ability damage as calculated in the "Calculating ability damage" section on the RS3 wiki (Min:1, Max:10000).

Stationary: Assumes the dummy is stuck in place, meaning bleed damage won't be multiplied. When not ticked, bleed damage is multiplied for all hits (unless the dummy is stunned and/or bound).

Stun&Bind Immune: Assumes the dummy is immune to stuns and binds from abilities.

Targets: Number of targets within reach for all AoE abilities.

Simulation T(s) and Start Adrenaline When a value has been set for both the Simulation Time (in seconds, min:1, max:3600) and the Starting Adrenaline (min:0, max:100), the Python script won't detect cycles but instead runs for the given simulation time and determines the Damage Per Tick (DPT) in that time.

Print more info: When ticked, up to the first 3 minutes of the simulation time can be verified upon clicking the resulting AADPT number.
